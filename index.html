Digital Transformation Aligned with Government Initiatives

The healthcare system in India is slowly moving towards digitization, but the organ transplant process remains largely manual and paper-based, which limits efficiency and scalability.

Centralized Platform for Organ Donation

The organ donation process in India is fragmented, with hospitals working in silos, which leads to inefficiencies, delays, and underutilization of available organs. This results in a vast number of organs going to waste and patients waiting unnecessarily long for transplants.

Learn More

Real-Time Matching of Donors and Recipients

Time is a critical factor in organ transplants. The window for using organs like hearts and lungs is very short (4-6 hours), but manual processes in place today lead to delays, making it difficult to match donors with recipients in time. This causes a high rate of organ wastage.

Reducing Middlemen and Exploitation

A centralized online platform would reduce the involvement of middlemen by directly connecting registered donors with hospitals. By streamlining the donor-registration process and digitally verifying records, it minimizes opportunities for fraud.

Organ Shortage Crisis

  • India’s Organ Donation Rate is Low

    According to the National Organ and Tissue Transplant Organization (NOTTO), India has an organ donation rate of 0.52 per million people, compared to Spain, which has 46.9 per million.

  • Thousands Awaiting Transplants

    As per government data, more than 500,000 people in India are estimated to be in need of organs annually, but only 2-3% of them receive a transplant.

  • Wastage of Available Organs

    A 2019 report from NOTTO revealed that nearly 60% of available organs go to waste due to logistical challenges in matching donors and recipients in time. Linking hospitals via a website could significantly reduce this waste.
